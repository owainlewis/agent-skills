# Image-to-Video Prompting Guide

This reference provides comprehensive guidance for animating images using Veo 3.1's image-to-video capabilities. Load this when working with uploaded images or when users want to animate existing visuals.

## Overview

Veo 3.1's image-to-video feature brings static images to life with enhanced prompt adherence and audio-visual quality. This works with:
- User-uploaded photos and artwork
- Images generated by Imagen or Gemini 2.5 Flash Image
- Existing visual assets from any source

## Core Principles

### 1. Describe What's Visible
Reference elements already present in the source image to maintain consistency.

**Example**:
- Image shows: Woman in red dress standing in garden
- Good prompt: "The woman in the red dress walks slowly through the garden, looking at flowers"
- Poor prompt: "A woman in blue walks through a forest" (contradicts image)

### 2. Specify the Action
Focus your prompt on what should move or change, not what's already there.

**Example**:
- Image shows: Chef standing at prep station
- Prompt focus: "The chef chops vegetables on the cutting board, their hands moving skillfully"

### 3. Align with Source Composition
Ensure actions make sense given the framing and perspective of the source image.

**Example**:
- Image shows: Close-up of face
- Good prompt: "She smiles and her eyes light up with joy"
- Poor prompt: "She walks across the room" (can't walk in a face close-up)

### 4. Use Subject Identifiers for Multiple People
When multiple subjects appear, use distinguishing descriptors.

**Example**:
- Image shows: Man in red hat and woman in green dress
- Prompt: "The man in the red hat says, 'Where is the rabbit?' Then the woman in the green dress next to him replies, 'There, in the woods.'"

## Analyzing Source Images

Before writing your prompt, identify:

1. **Main subjects**: Who or what is in the frame?
2. **Composition**: Close-up, medium shot, wide shot?
3. **Setting**: Indoor, outdoor, what environment?
4. **Existing pose/state**: Standing, sitting, static object?
5. **Perspective**: Eye level, low angle, aerial?
6. **Distinguishing features**: Colors, clothing, objects that identify subjects

## Prompt Patterns for Different Image Types

### Portrait Close-Ups

**Image characteristics**: Face fills frame, minimal background

**Effective actions**:
- Facial expressions changing
- Eyes moving or blinking
- Subtle head movements
- Hair moving in wind
- Speaking dialogue

**Example prompt**:
"Close-up of the woman's face as her expression shifts from neutral to a warm smile, her eyes crinkling with genuine joy. Her hair moves slightly in a gentle breeze. SFX: Soft wind, distant birds chirping."

### Medium Shots (Waist Up)

**Image characteristics**: Subject from waist up, some background visible

**Effective actions**:
- Upper body movements
- Hand gestures
- Turning head or torso
- Interacting with nearby objects
- Speaking with body language

**Example prompt**:
"The man in the blue shirt leans forward and gestures with his hands while speaking, his expression animated and engaged. The background café scene remains softly blurred."

### Wide Shots

**Image characteristics**: Full subject(s) and significant environment

**Effective actions**:
- Walking or moving through space
- Environmental elements animating
- Multiple subjects interacting
- Camera subtle movement
- Atmospheric effects

**Example prompt**:
"Wide shot of the couple walking hand in hand along the beach path toward the sunset, waves gently rolling onto shore, their shadows stretching across the sand. Peaceful and romantic mood with warm golden tones."

### Product Shots

**Image characteristics**: Object on simple background

**Effective actions**:
- Camera movements around product
- Subtle product rotation
- Environmental effects (mist, light changes)
- Elements appearing (like liquid pouring)

**Example prompt**:
"Slow 360-degree orbit around the watch on the pedestal, dramatic side lighting creating reflections and highlights on the polished metal surface, premium commercial aesthetic."

### Landscape/Nature Scenes

**Image characteristics**: Natural environment, no main subject

**Effective actions**:
- Weather effects (clouds moving, rain)
- Water flowing or waves
- Trees swaying
- Light changing
- Camera panning or moving

**Example prompt**:
"The waterfall flows powerfully down the rocks, mist rising and catching the sunlight, surrounding trees swaying gently in the breeze. Natural ambient sounds of rushing water and forest."

## Workflow: From Image Analysis to Prompt

### Step 1: Describe What You See
Make mental or written notes of image contents.

**Example observation**: "Close-up of elderly man's hands holding a vintage compass, wooden table surface visible, warm indoor lighting"

### Step 2: Decide What Should Animate
Choose actions that fit the composition and make narrative sense.

**Animation decision**: Hands slowly rotating the compass, examining it

### Step 3: Add Context and Mood
Layer in atmospheric details and emotional tone.

**Context addition**: Contemplative mood, perhaps remembering past journeys

### Step 4: Specify Audio (if desired)
Add synchronized sound that enhances the scene.

**Audio addition**: Gentle metallic click of compass opening, quiet ambient room tone

### Step 5: Construct Complete Prompt
Combine all elements using the five-part formula adapted for image-to-video.

**Final prompt**: "Close-up of the elderly man's weathered hands as he slowly rotates the vintage brass compass, examining it thoughtfully. His fingers trace the worn surface with familiarity. Warm indoor lighting emphasizes the texture of both the compass and his hands. Contemplative and nostalgic mood. SFX: Soft metallic click, quiet room ambiance."

## Common Image-to-Video Scenarios

### Scenario 1: Portrait with Dialogue

**Image**: Person facing camera, neutral expression
**Goal**: Add speech and expression

**Prompt structure**:
1. Describe subject (reference visible clothing/features)
2. Specify expression change
3. Add exact dialogue in quotes
4. Include vocal quality descriptor

**Example**: "The woman in the blue sweater's face lights up with recognition as she says warmly, 'I remember you!' Her eyes widen slightly and a genuine smile spreads across her face."

### Scenario 2: Action Continuation

**Image**: Person mid-movement or poised to move
**Goal**: Complete or extend the action

**Prompt structure**:
1. Reference current pose/position
2. Describe the continuation of movement
3. Specify movement quality (slowly, energetically, etc.)
4. Add environmental context

**Example**: "The runner continues their stride along the beach path, moving energetically past the palm trees. Their ponytail bounces with each step. Morning sunlight creates long shadows. SFX: Footsteps on sand, distant ocean waves."

### Scenario 3: Environmental Animation

**Image**: Static scene with potential for atmospheric movement
**Goal**: Add life through environmental effects

**Prompt structure**:
1. Identify animatable elements (water, smoke, plants, etc.)
2. Specify how they should move
3. Add camera movement if appropriate
4. Include ambient audio

**Example**: "The neon signs in the urban alley flicker and glow, steam rises from a street grate, light rain creates reflections on the wet pavement. The camera slowly pans right revealing more of the atmospheric street scene. SFX: Distant traffic, rain hitting pavement, neon buzzing."

### Scenario 4: Product Reveal

**Image**: Product in interesting composition
**Goal**: Showcase through movement and lighting

**Prompt structure**:
1. Describe camera movement around product
2. Specify lighting changes or highlights
3. Add premium/commercial feel descriptors
4. Optional: Add subtle background animation

**Example**: "Smooth tracking shot orbits the perfume bottle on marble, revealing elegant curves and transparency. Soft mist disperses around the bottle catching backlight. Luxurious and refined commercial aesthetic."

## Advanced Techniques

### Technique 1: Subtle Character Moments

For portraits, small authentic movements create engaging results:
- Breathing (slight chest rise and fall)
- Natural blinking
- Micro-expressions
- Hair or fabric movement
- Weight shifts

**Example**: "The young woman in the portrait breathes naturally, her expression serene but her eyes showing deep thought. A slight breeze moves strands of her hair. Natural and contemplative."

### Technique 2: Matching Image Aesthetic

Maintain visual consistency with the source image style:
- If image is grainy film, mention "film grain aesthetic"
- If image is high-key lit, specify "maintaining bright, even lighting"
- If image has specific color grade, reference it

**Example**: "Maintaining the vintage 1970s film aesthetic of the image, the band members begin to move in rhythm, the lead singer swaying to an unheard beat. Warm amber tones and slight grain preserved throughout."

### Technique 3: Progressive Reveals

Use camera movement to reveal elements beyond the frame:
- Dolly back to show more context
- Pan to reveal additional subjects
- Tilt up/down to show related elements

**Example**: "Camera slowly dollies back from the close-up of the architect's hands drawing on blueprint, gradually revealing the full drafting table, then the architect's focused face, then the sunlit studio beyond. Documentary style with natural progression."

### Technique 4: Emotional Arcs

For character shots, specify emotional progression:

**Example**: "The soldier's stern expression in the photograph slowly softens as a small smile forms at the corners of his mouth, his eyes moistening slightly with emotion as if reacting to an off-camera reunion. Tender and moving moment."

## Troubleshooting Common Issues

### Issue: Animation doesn't match image composition

**Cause**: Requested action impossible given the framing
**Solution**: Match action scope to image framing - small movements for close-ups, larger actions for wide shots

### Issue: Subject appearance changes from source

**Cause**: Insufficient reference to source image details
**Solution**: Explicitly describe visible clothing, features, colors to maintain consistency

### Issue: Multiple subjects confused

**Cause**: Ambiguous subject references
**Solution**: Use specific descriptors: "the woman in the yellow dress" not just "the woman"

### Issue: Unnatural or jerky movement

**Cause**: Action description unclear or too complex
**Solution**: Simplify to 1-2 clear movements, add "smooth motion" descriptor

### Issue: Background changes unexpectedly

**Cause**: Not anchoring to visible background elements
**Solution**: Reference specific background details: "with the brick wall behind her" or "maintaining the blurred café background"

## Example Transformations

### Example 1: Still Portrait to Animated Portrait

**Source Image**: Professional headshot of businesswoman in office, neutral expression, natural window lighting

**Basic Prompt**: "She smiles."

**Enhanced Prompt**: "The businesswoman's professional demeanor softens as a warm, genuine smile spreads across her face, her eyes reflecting confidence and approachability. The natural window lighting remains consistent, highlighting her features. Professional and personable mood."

**Why better**: Describes the emotional quality, maintains lighting consistency, adds professional context

### Example 2: Product Photo to Commercial Shot

**Source Image**: Luxury watch on black velvet background, straight-on view

**Basic Prompt**: "The watch rotates."

**Enhanced Prompt**: "Slow 360-degree rotation of the luxury watch on the velvet surface, revealing intricate details of the mechanical movement through the transparent case back, polished metal catching dramatic side lighting creating highlights and reflections. Premium commercial aesthetic, shallow depth of field maintaining focus on the timepiece."

**Why better**: Specifies smooth movement, describes what's revealed, adds lighting and aesthetic details

### Example 3: Landscape to Dynamic Scene

**Source Image**: Mountain valley at dawn, mist in valley, peaks visible

**Basic Prompt**: "Clouds move."

**Enhanced Prompt**: "Time-lapse effect as morning mist slowly disperses through the mountain valley, clouds drifting past distant peaks, the rising sun gradually illuminating the landscape with warm golden light. Smooth camera tilt up from valley floor to mountain peaks. Epic natural documentary style with peaceful atmosphere. SFX: Gentle wind, distant bird calls."

**Why better**: Describes specific atmospheric changes, adds camera movement, includes audio, specifies mood

## Integration with Other Workflows

### Using with Gemini 2.5 Flash Image

1. Generate ideal starting frame with Gemini
2. Use that image as source for Veo animation
3. Perfect control over both composition and animation

### Using with "First and Last Frame"

1. Start with existing image as first frame
2. Generate or use another image as last frame
3. Veo creates transition between them
4. Perfect for controlled camera moves or transformations

### Using with "Ingredients to Video"

1. Use source image as character/style reference
2. Generate video with that reference maintained
3. Ensures consistency across multiple shots

## Remember

- Image-to-video is about bringing existing compositions to life
- Reference what's visible to maintain consistency
- Match action complexity to clip length and framing
- Use specific descriptors for multiple subjects
- Audio can greatly enhance image-to-video results
- Start simple and add complexity as needed
